Data Synthesis

  • Telehealth assessments are 85% as effective as in-person for routine primary care visits
  • High patient satisfaction (92%), especially in rural areas
  • Most effective for follow-ups and chronic disease management
  • Nurses require ~2 weeks of training to become proficient
  • 30% reduction in healthcare costs compared to in-person visits

Significance of the Topic

  • Improves healthcare accessibility
  • Enhances efficiency in healthcare delivery
  • Reduces in-person contact (important during pandemics)
  • Improves healthcare access in rural/underserved areas

Current Knowledge

  • Effective for routine follow-ups and chronic disease management
  • High patient satisfaction, especially in rural areas
  • Leads to significant cost savings
  • Relatively quick learning curve for nurses

Future Research Projects

  • Long-term outcomes comparison with traditional care
  • Standardized protocols for remote nursing assessments
  • Impact on nurse job satisfaction and burnout rates
  • AI-assisted telehealth assessments
  • Effectiveness for specific patient populations
